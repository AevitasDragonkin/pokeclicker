The Battle Tree is an endurance-style challenge where you assemble a small team of Pokémon and challenge a progressively harder Battle Climb. Your goal is to reach the highest platform you can by making smart team choices, selecting powerful modifiers, and managing your lineup’s survivability.
Every attempt through the Battle Tree is deterministic: if you make the same choices, you will see the same outcomes. However, even a single different decision can change the rest of your Battle Climb dramatically.

Team selection

At the start of each Battle Climb:

  • You may choose 1 to 3 Pokémon.
  • Your options come from a rotating daily pool of 20 Pokémon.
  • The daily pool is chosen from a set of themed categories
    (e.g., Pure Fire, Kanto, Galar, Grass…).

Team Order Matters

Your Pokémon fight in the order you placed them:

  • The first non-fainted Pokémon on your team is always sent into battle.
  • It continues fighting until it faints.
  • If a Pokémon has been revived while another is currently active, it will re-enter once the current battler faints.

You cannot swap Pokémon or manually change the active battler during the climb.

Pokémon Levels

  • All your Pokémon use your Battle Tree Level (capped at 100).
  • Some modifiers may increase their level past the level 100 cap during the Battle Climb.
Battles

Each platform contains an opposing team of 3 Pokémon.

  • Enemy Pokémon levels are equal to the platform number.
    (Platform 46 = level 46 enemies.)
  • Enemy levels are uncapped, and scale indefinitely.

After you defeat all three enemy Pokémon, you advance one platform. Your Battle Climb continues until all your Pokémon have fainted.

Modifiers
  • Every 5 platforms, you will be offered 3 modifiers, and you must select one to continue
  • Some modifiers only appear if certain requirements are met
  • Modifiers apply for the rest of your Battle Climb once selected

For a list of all the modifiers, see the 'Modifiers' tab.

Rewards

There are three types of rewards:

  • One-Time

    Rewards that can only be earned once, claimable from the Rewards Tab.

  • Daily

    Rewards that can be claimed once per day from the Rewards Tab.

  • Battle Climb

    These rewards are automatically collected during your Battle Climb once the requirements are met, but stored until you finish your Battle Climb.
    You only receive them after your Battle Climb ends.

    • They are affected by your Reward Multiplier
    • After multiplier adjustments, values are rounded down.
      Example: 1 × 0.90 = 0.

    You may optionally disable auto-claim in the settings.

Any reward type may have a requirement, such as:

  • Reach a certain Battle Tree Level
  • Defeat a specific daily platform
  • Complete a number of Battle Climbs total or in a single day
Ending a Battle Climb

Ending your Battle Climb happens in the following scenarios:

  • All of your Pokémon have fainted
  • You forfeited the Battle Climb
  • You selected a modifier that has ended your Battle Climb
Deterministic

Every Battle Climb is built from a deterministic sequence of outcomes.
If two players make the same choices, they will encounter the same battles, modifiers, and results.
Changing any decision — choosing a different Pokémon, selecting a different modifier, etc. — will alter the rest of the Battle Climb.
